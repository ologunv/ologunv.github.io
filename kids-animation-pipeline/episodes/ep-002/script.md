# Episode 002 Script — Lulu and the Bedtime Stars

## Lesson
Bedtime routines help us feel calm and ready for sleep.

## Runtime Target
3.5 minutes

## Scene 1
- Setting: Lulu's room at evening
- Visual action: Lulu flies from toy to toy, still full of energy.
- Dialogue:
  - Lulu: "I'm not sleepy yet!"
  - Lulu: "One more game!"
  - Mimi: "The stars are coming out."
- Narration: Lulu was having so much fun that bedtime felt very far away.
- Music/SFX: Soft evening music

## Scene 2
- Setting: Same room
- Visual action: Mimi makes a bedtime plan with playful motions.
- Dialogue:
  - Mimi: "Let's do bedtime step by step."
  - Mimi: "First, we tidy up."
  - Lulu: "Like a game?"
  - Mimi: "Exactly."
- Narration: Mimi had an idea. What if bedtime could feel like a gentle little game?
- Music/SFX: Light magical chime

## Scene 3
- Setting: Bathroom and bedroom transitions
- Visual action: Lulu puts away toys, brushes teeth, and puts on pajamas.
- Dialogue:
  - Lulu: "Toys away! Brush, brush, brush!"
  - Mimi: "Great job, Lulu."
  - Lulu: "My pajamas are cozy!"
- Narration: One small step at a time, Lulu started to feel calm.
- Music/SFX: Soft brushing sounds, cozy music

## Scene 4
- Setting: Bedroom window and bed
- Visual action: Lulu and Mimi count stars, then Lulu snuggles into bed.
- Dialogue:
  - Mimi: "Let's count the stars together."
  - Lulu: "One, two, three..."
  - Lulu: "Now I do feel sleepy."
  - Mimi: "Good night, Lulu."
- Narration: Bedtime was not scary or boring. It was soft, calm, and full of gentle little steps.
- Music/SFX: Night ambience, tiny twinkles

## Ending Recap
- Lesson summary: Bedtime routines help our bodies and minds get ready for rest.
- Final upbeat line: "Good night, stars."
